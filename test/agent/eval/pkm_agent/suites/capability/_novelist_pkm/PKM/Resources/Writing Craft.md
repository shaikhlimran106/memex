Running file for craft-level observations. Not chapter-specific.

## On voice

- Saunders: "voice is what survives revision". Keep cutting until
  what's left can only be said by this narrator.
- The footnote gimmick works as long as it's earning the joke. The
  moment the footnote *is* the joke, kill it.

## On pacing

- Chapters that open in the middle of a sentence are usually a
  symptom, not a feature.
- The "and then" test: if every paragraph could begin with "and
  then", the scene is sequence, not story.

## On revision

- First pass: cut 10%.
- Second pass: cut 10% of what's left.
- Third pass: ask whether each scene is doing two jobs at once.
